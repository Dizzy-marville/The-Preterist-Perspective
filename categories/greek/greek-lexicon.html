Greek Lexicon — Grammar of Fulfillment

“Till heaven and earth pass, one jot or one tittle shall in no wise pass from the law, till all be fulfilled.”

Parousia (παρουσία) — Presence, arrival, royal visitation. The king inspects. The judgment speaks. The scroll opens.

Aion (αἰών) — Age, world, sacred duration. The epoch ends. The geometry shifts. The covenant expands.

Orgē (ὀργή) — Wrath, ripening, covenant consequence. The patience ends. The verdict arrives. The fire descends.

Mystērion (μυστήριον) — Hidden meaning, sacred disclosure. The veil lifts. The silence speaks. The mystery unveils.

This scroll binds the shelf in grammar. The Greek is not ornamental—it is foundational. The shelf does not just explain—it fulfills. The prophecy breathes. The language sings. The scroll closes.
